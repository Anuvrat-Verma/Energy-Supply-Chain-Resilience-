SOURCE: HDFC Securities Institutional Equity Research Report

[CHOKEPOINT TELEMETRY]
The US-Israel vs Iran war has forced the absolute closure of the Strait of Hormuz, cutting production across Iraq, Saudi Arabia, and Kuwait. More than 50% of India's crude oil imports flow through this single point. Brent Crude has surged from USD 73/bbl to past USD 90/bbl. 

[OMC MARKETING MARGIN CRUSH]
Indian retail fuel prices remain unchanged at the pump. Consequently, Oil Marketing Company (OMC) integrated margins are collapsing below the 9MFY26 baseline of INR 8/ltr to INR 12/ltr. Every INR 1/ltr drop in marketing margin contracts FY27 EPS by: IOCL (-21%), BPCL (-20%), and HPCL (-24%).

[REFINING AND CRACK SPREADS DATA]
Due to sudden trade dislocations, product cracks have expanded massively in March 2026. Gasoline cracks rose to USD 34/bbl (+70%) and Diesel cracks hit USD 25.4/bbl (a 2.8x surge). Singapore Gross Refining Margins (GRMs) expanded 91% to USD 19.4/bbl from a USD 10.2/bbl baseline. For every USD 1/bbl gain in GRM, annual EPS gains are: IOCL (+11%), BPCL (+9%), and HPCL (+7%).

[UTILITY AND UPSTREAM DIVERGENCE]
City Gas Distribution (CGD) configurations are failing due to the total shutdown of Qatar's Ras Laffan LNG plant, causing restricted gas availability and high spot prices alongside a weakening Rupee. Upstream developers gain: every USD 5/bbl crude spike above USD 70/bbl expands ONGC standalone annual EPS by 14% and Oil India by 9%.
